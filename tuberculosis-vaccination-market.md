Immune vaccines aim to prevent the onset of tuberculosis by stimulating the body's immune response to fight off the disease before infection occurs. Therapy vaccines, on the other hand, are designed to treat individuals who are already infected with tuberculosis by boosting their immune response to eliminate the bacteria. Both types of vaccines play a crucial role in the global efforts to control and eradicate tuberculosis.</p></p>
