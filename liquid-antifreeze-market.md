<p><p>Liquid antifreeze market types include alcohols, alcohol ether, chlorinated hydrocarbons, and other markets. Alcohols, such as ethylene glycol and propylene glycol, are commonly used in automotive antifreeze due to their high performance and low cost. Alcohol ethers, like ethylene glycol methyl ether, offer improved solvency and lower toxicity compared to alcohols. Chlorinated hydrocarbons, such as methylene chloride, are effective in industrial applications but are facing regulatory restrictions. Other markets may include environmentally friendly alternatives like bio-based antifreeze or non-toxic options for specific applications.</p></p>
