<p><p>Copper cable testers come in two main types: analog and digital. Analog testers provide simple pass/fail results, while digital testers offer more detailed and accurate analysis of cable quality. In the market, analog testers are typically more cost-effective and easier to use, making them popular for basic testing needs. On the other hand, digital testers are favored by professionals and organizations that require advanced features like distance to fault measurement and signal strength analysis. Both types cater to different user requirements and budget considerations within the copper cable testing market.</p></p>
