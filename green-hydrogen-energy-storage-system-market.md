<p><p>Green hydrogen energy storage systems are categorized into three types based on their physical state: gas, liquid, and solid. Gas storage systems involve storing hydrogen gas in high-pressure tanks, making it suitable for large-scale industrial applications. Liquid storage systems involve storing hydrogen in cryogenic tanks at low temperatures, allowing for a higher density of storage. Solid storage systems involve storing hydrogen in solid-state materials such as metal hydrides or chemical compounds, providing a safe and compact storage solution for transportation and stationary applications.</p></p>
