<p><p>Automotive powder coating refers to the process of applying a dry powder on a surface of automobile components using electrostatic charge. Automotive powder coating offers both aesthetic benefits and provides protection to various automotive parts such as wheels, chassis, bumpers, and engine components.</p><p>The global automotive powder coating market is expected to witness significant growth in the coming years, with a projected CAGR of 11.2% during the forecast period. Additionally, technological advancements in powder coating formulations, such as improved scratch resistance and color options, are further fueling the demand for automotive powder coatings. Manufacturers are also focusing on developing innovative powder coating solutions that offer enhanced performance and aesthetics to meet the evolving needs of the automotive industry.</p></p>

<p><p>Automotive powder coating market types include polyester/TGIC powder coating, acrylics powder coating, polyester/urethane powder coating, hybrid powder coating, epoxy powder coating, and others. Polyester/TGIC powder coating offers excellent weather resistance, while acrylics powder coating provides a smooth finish and good color retention. Polyester/urethane powder coating combines the durability of polyester with the flexibility of urethane. Hybrid powder coating combines epoxy and polyester to offer both corrosion resistance and impact resistance. Epoxy powder coating is known for its chemical resistance and durability. Other types of coatings may offer unique properties or applications in the automotive industry.</p></p>
