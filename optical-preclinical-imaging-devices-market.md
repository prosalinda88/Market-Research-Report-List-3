<p><p>Optical preclinical imaging devices are utilized for non-invasive imaging of small animals in research and drug development. Two common types are fluorescence and bioluminescence imaging. Fluorescence imaging involves tagging molecules with fluorescent dyes to track biological processes, while bioluminescence imaging uses light-emitting enzymes to visualize gene expression. These technologies provide insights into disease progression, drug efficacy, and molecular pathways. The market for these devices continues to expand as researchers seek faster, more accurate, and cost-effective imaging solutions.</p></p>
