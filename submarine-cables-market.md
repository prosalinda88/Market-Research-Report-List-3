<p><ul><li>Impregnated Paper Cable</li><li>Self-Contained Oil-Filled Cable</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Submarine cables are high-voltage cables used for transmitting power across bodies of water. Two common types are Impregnated Paper Cables, which are insulated with impregnated paper for underwater use, and Self-Contained Oil-Filled Cables, which use oil as insulation and cooling. Impregnated Paper Cables are cost-effective but require regular maintenance, while Self-Contained Oil-Filled Cables are more expensive but offer better performance and longer lifespan. Both types are essential for powering offshore installations and interconnecting electrical grids across seas.</p></p>
