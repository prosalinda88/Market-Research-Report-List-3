<p><p>Textile pigments are categorized into different types based on their chemical composition and properties. The main types include disperse pigments, which are suitable for coloring synthetic fibers; reactive pigments, which form a chemical bond with the fabric; sulfur pigments, known for their high wash fastness; VAT pigments, which are water-insoluble and require a reducing agent for fixation; acid pigments, which are commonly used for dyeing wool and silk; and other miscellaneous types. Each type offers unique benefits and is used in various textile dyeing and printing applications.</p></p>
